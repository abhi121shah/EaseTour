Culinary Guide

10 Iconic Kerala Dishes

A curated selection of the most authentic and beloved culinary masterpieces from the Malabar coast to the backwaters.

  • Appam with Ishtu (Stew)

    Fermented rice pancakes with a soft, fluffy center and lacy edges, served with a delicate coconut milk gravy.

  • Karimeen Pollichathu

    Pearl spot fish marinated in traditional Malabar spices, wrapped in a banana leaf and charcoal-grilled to perfection.

  • Kerala Sadya

    A grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf featuring over 24 traditional dishes including Avial and Sambar.

  • Puttu and Kadala Curry

    A breakfast staple of steamed rice cakes layered with coconut, paired with a spicy black chickpea curry.

  • Malabar Parotta with Beef Fry

    Flaky, multi-layered flatbread served with slow-roasted beef pieces tossed in aromatic spices and coconut oil.

  • Kerala Prawn Curry

    Juicy prawns simmered in a creamy coconut milk base infused with Malabar tamarind and fresh curry leaves.

  • Thalassery Biriyani

    Unique Malabar biriyani made with short-grain Kaima rice, pure ghee, and a rich masala blend.

  • Idiyappam

    Also known as string hoppers, these delicate rice noodles are steamed and typically paired with spicy egg curry.

  • Kozhikodan Halwa

    A famous flour-based sweet from Kozhikode, cooked in coconut oil and flavored with cardamom and cashews.

  • Banana Chips

    Slices of raw Nendran bananas fried in pure coconut oil, seasoned with turmeric and salt—a quintessential Kerala snack.
