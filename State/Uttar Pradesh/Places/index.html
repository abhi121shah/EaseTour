The Landmarks of Uttar Pradesh

Embark on a journey through the heart of India's heritage, where ancient spirituality meets architectural grandeur in every stone and river bank.

Iconic Sites

Discover the hand-picked historical and spiritual wonders that define the soul of the North.

Taj Mahal ( Agra )

A white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Prem Mandir ( Vrindavan )

A serene temple dedicated to Radha-Krishna, attracting visitors with its beauty and divine ambiance.

Fatehpur Sikri

A short-lived capital of the Mughal Empire, showcasing a blend of Hindu and Persian architecture.

Kashi Vishwanath ( Varanasi )

One of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the sacred city of Varanasi.

Augarhnath Mandir ( Meerut )

A historic temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, believed to be linked with the Revolt of 1857 and known for its peaceful spiritual atmosphere.

Bara Imambara ( Lucknow )

An architectural marvel in Lucknow, famous for its incredible 'Bhulbhulaiya' (labyrinth) and massive unsupported hall.

Ayodhya Ram Mandir

A magnificent temple believed to be the birthplace of Lord Ram, attracting millions of devotees.

Sarnath ( Varanasi )

A sacred place for Buddhists where Lord Buddha first taught the Dharma after attaining enlightenment.

Sangam ( Prayagraj )

Triveni Sangam in Prayagraj, the sacred meeting point of the Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati.

Jhansi Fort

A famous fort symbolizing courage and the heroic legacy of Rani Lakshmibai.
