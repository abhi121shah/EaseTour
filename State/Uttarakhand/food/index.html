The Flavors of Uttarakhand

Discover the spice-scented heritage of the mountains, where every dish tells a story of tradition, purity, and the rugged beauty of the Himalayas.

10 Iconic Uttarakhand Dishes

A curated selection of the most authentic and beloved tastes from the heart of the Himalayas.

star 4.9

Kafuli

A nutritious green gravy made of spinach and fenugreek leaves, cooked in traditional iron kadhais to preserve its mineral essence.

star 4.7

Phanu

A complex dish prepared by soaking lentils overnight and grinding them into a paste before slow-cooking with mountain spices.

star 4.6

Baadi

Made from Kwada flour, this simple yet iconic staple is best enjoyed hot with a dollop of fresh mountain ghee and Phanu.

star 4.8

Kandalee Ka Saag

A unique delicacy made from stinging nettle leaves (Kandali), carefully prepared to remove the sting while retaining its earthy flavor.

star 4.5

Chainsoo

High-protein roasted black gram dal, ground and slow-cooked in iron vessels to achieve its signature dark color and smoky aroma.

star 4.9

Kumaoni Raita

A refreshing yogurt-based side dish prepared with cucumber, turmeric, and the distinctive punch of local mustard seeds.

star 4.4

Dubuk

A winter favorite made with local pulses like Gahat or Bhatt, ground into a paste and simmered with aromatic hill spices.

star 4.8

Gulgula

Sweet, golden-brown fried balls made from wheat flour and jaggery, serving as the perfect tea-time snack in the mountains.

star 4.7

Arsa

A festive sweet essential for weddings, made of rice flour and jaggery syrup, fried to a perfect chewy consistency.

star 5.0

Bal Mithai

The pride of Almora, this dark brown chocolate-like fudge is made from roasted khoya and coated in white sugar balls.
