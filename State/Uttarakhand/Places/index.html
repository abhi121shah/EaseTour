The Landmarks of Uttarakhand

Where the mountains touch the heavens—explore the timeless grace of the "Land of the Gods".

Rishikesh

A spiritual haven where the Ganges descends from the Himalayas, offering a blend of ancient wisdom and white-water adventure.

Mussoorie

Perched on a horseshoe-shaped ridge, this colonial-era hill station offers breathtaking views of the Shivalik range and Doon Valley.

Nainital

A sparkling emerald lake surrounded by seven hills, famous for its pear-shaped water body and misty mountain charm.

Auli

India's premier skiing destination offering panoramic views of Nanda Devi and pristine white slopes for winter enthusiasts.

Valley of Flowers

A high-altitude Himalayan valley that comes alive with hundreds of species of wildflowers during the monsoon months.

Badrinath Temple

One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, dedicated to Lord Vishnu and nestled between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges.

Kedarnath Temple

A remote and sacred stone temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, accessible only by a trek through the majestic Garhwal Himalayas.

Jim Corbett

India's oldest national park, home to the majestic Bengal tiger and diverse flora and fauna in the foothills of the Himalayas.

Haridwar

One of the holiest places in India, where the celestial Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri creates a mystical atmosphere of light and sound.

Hemkund Sahib

A high-altitude Sikh pilgrimage site situated beside a crystal clear glacial lake surrounded by seven snow-clad peaks.
